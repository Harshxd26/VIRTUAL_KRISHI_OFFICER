Crop Cultivation Guide - Comprehensive Agricultural Knowledge

WHEAT CULTIVATION
Wheat (Triticum aestivum) is one of the most important cereal crops grown worldwide. In India, wheat is primarily a Rabi crop, sown in October-November and harvested in March-April.

Climate Requirements:
Wheat grows best in cool, moist conditions during the growing season and warm, dry weather during ripening. The ideal temperature range is 10-25°C. The crop requires about 450-650mm of rainfall, well-distributed throughout the growing period.

Soil Requirements:
Wheat can be grown on a variety of soils, but well-drained loamy soils with good fertility are ideal. The soil pH should be between 6.5 and 7.5. Good drainage is essential as waterlogging can severely damage the crop.

Sowing Time:
- Early sowing: Mid-October to end of October (for timely sown wheat)
- Normal sowing: First fortnight of November
- Late sowing: After November 25 (reduce seed rate by 25%)

Seed Rate:
- Normal conditions: 100 kg/ha
- Late sowing: 125 kg/ha
- Irrigated conditions: 100-125 kg/ha

Irrigation:
Wheat requires 5-6 irrigations:
1. Crown root initiation (20-25 days after sowing)
2. Tillering stage (40-45 DAS)
3. Jointing stage (60-65 DAS)
4. Flowering stage (80-85 DAS)
5. Milk stage (100-105 DAS)
6. Dough stage (115-120 DAS)

Fertilizer Management:
Apply N:P:K in the ratio of 120:60:40 kg/ha for high-yielding varieties. Full dose of P and K at sowing. Nitrogen in 2-3 split doses.

Common Varieties:
HD-2967, PBW-343, WH-542, DBW-17, HD-3086, HD-3226

RICE CULTIVATION
Rice (Oryza sativa) is the staple food for more than half of the world's population. In India, it is grown in both Kharif and Rabi seasons.

Climate Requirements:
Rice requires high temperature, high humidity, and abundant rainfall. The ideal temperature is 20-35°C. It needs 150-200 cm of annual rainfall.

Soil Requirements:
Rice can be grown on a wide variety of soils, but clay loam soils with good water retention capacity are ideal. The soil pH should be between 5.5 and 6.5.

Sowing Time:
- Kharif rice: June-July (monsoon season)
- Rabi rice: November-December (winter season)

Seed Rate:
- Direct seeding: 80-100 kg/ha
- Transplanting: 20-25 kg/ha for nursery

Water Management:
Rice requires continuous standing water. Maintain 5-7 cm water depth during vegetative phase and 2-3 cm during reproductive phase.

Fertilizer Management:
Apply N:P:K in the ratio of 100:50:50 kg/ha. Apply nitrogen in 3-4 split doses.

Common Varieties:
Pusa Basmati 1121, Pusa Basmati 1509, Swarna, MTU 1010, BPT 5204

TOMATO CULTIVATION
Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum) is one of the most important vegetable crops grown worldwide.

Climate Requirements:
Tomato grows best in warm, sunny conditions. The ideal temperature range is 18-27°C. It requires moderate rainfall (600-1000 mm).

Soil Requirements:
Well-drained, fertile loamy soil with pH 6.0-7.0 is ideal. Sandy loam soils are also suitable.

Sowing Time:
- Kharif: June-July
- Rabi: October-November
- Summer: January-February (in protected cultivation)

Seed Rate:
- Direct sowing: 400-500 g/ha
- Transplanting: 200-300 g/ha for nursery

Spacing:
- Row to row: 60-75 cm
- Plant to plant: 45-60 cm

Irrigation:
Tomato requires regular irrigation. Water requirement is 400-600 mm. Avoid water stress during flowering and fruit development stages.

Fertilizer Management:
Apply N:P:K in the ratio of 120:80:80 kg/ha. Apply organic manure at 20-25 tonnes/ha.

Common Varieties:
Pusa Ruby, Arka Vikas, Arka Saurabh, Pusa Gaurav, Arka Meghali

POTATO CULTIVATION
Potato (Solanum tuberosum) is the world's most important non-cereal food crop.

Climate Requirements:
Potato grows best in cool climates. The ideal temperature range is 15-25°C. It requires moderate rainfall (500-700 mm).

Soil Requirements:
Well-drained, loose, friable soil with pH 5.5-6.5 is ideal. Sandy loam to loam soils are preferred.

Sowing Time:
- Early: September-October
- Main: October-November
- Late: November-December

Seed Rate:
Use 2.5-3.0 tonnes/ha of certified seed tubers.

Spacing:
- Row to row: 50-60 cm
- Plant to plant: 20-25 cm

Irrigation:
Potato requires 5-6 irrigations. Critical stages are tuber initiation and tuber development.

Fertilizer Management:
Apply N:P:K in the ratio of 120:100:120 kg/ha. Apply organic manure at 20-25 tonnes/ha.

Common Varieties:
Kufri Chandramukhi, Kufri Jyoti, Kufri Pukhraj, Kufri Bahar

COTTON CULTIVATION
Cotton (Gossypium spp.) is an important fiber crop and cash crop.

Climate Requirements:
Cotton requires warm, sunny weather. The ideal temperature range is 21-30°C. It requires 500-700 mm of rainfall.

Soil Requirements:
Well-drained, deep, fertile loamy soils with pH 6.0-8.0 are ideal. Black cotton soil is highly suitable.

Sowing Time:
- Kharif: May-June (with onset of monsoon)
- Early sowing: April (in irrigated areas)

Seed Rate:
- Desi cotton: 8-10 kg/ha
- American cotton: 15-20 kg/ha

Spacing:
- Row to row: 60-90 cm
- Plant to plant: 30-45 cm

Irrigation:
Cotton requires 5-6 irrigations. Critical stages are flowering and boll development.

Fertilizer Management:
Apply N:P:K in the ratio of 100:50:50 kg/ha. Apply organic manure at 10-15 tonnes/ha.

Common Varieties:
Bollgard II, RCH 2, RCH 20, MRC 7017, Ankur 3028

SUGARCANE CULTIVATION
Sugarcane (Saccharum officinarum) is an important cash crop and source of sugar.

Climate Requirements:
Sugarcane requires hot, humid climate. The ideal temperature range is 26-32°C. It requires 1500-2000 mm of annual rainfall.

Soil Requirements:
Deep, well-drained, fertile loamy soils with pH 6.5-7.5 are ideal. Alluvial soils are highly suitable.

Planting Time:
- Spring planting: February-March
- Autumn planting: October-November

Seed Rate:
Use 35,000-40,000 three-budded setts per hectare.

Spacing:
- Row to row: 90-120 cm
- Plant to plant: 30-45 cm

Irrigation:
Sugarcane requires regular irrigation. Water requirement is 2000-2500 mm per crop cycle.

Fertilizer Management:
Apply N:P:K in the ratio of 200:80:80 kg/ha. Apply organic manure at 20-25 tonnes/ha.

Common Varieties:
Co 0238, Co 86032, Co 8371, Co 1148, Co 15023

MAIZE CULTIVATION
Maize (Zea mays) is an important cereal crop used for food, feed, and industrial purposes.

Climate Requirements:
Maize requires warm weather. The ideal temperature range is 18-27°C. It requires 500-800 mm of rainfall.

Soil Requirements:
Well-drained, fertile loamy soils with pH 6.0-7.5 are ideal.

Sowing Time:
- Kharif: June-July
- Rabi: October-November
- Spring: January-February

Seed Rate:
- Normal: 20-25 kg/ha
- Hybrid: 15-20 kg/ha

Spacing:
- Row to row: 60-75 cm
- Plant to plant: 20-25 cm

Irrigation:
Maize requires 4-5 irrigations. Critical stages are tasseling and grain filling.

Fertilizer Management:
Apply N:P:K in the ratio of 120:60:40 kg/ha. Apply organic manure at 10-15 tonnes/ha.

Common Varieties:
Hybrid Maize 900M Gold, Pusa Hybrid Makka 1, Pusa Hybrid Makka 2, HQPM 1

GENERAL CULTIVATION PRACTICES

Land Preparation:
Proper land preparation is essential for good crop establishment. It includes plowing, harrowing, leveling, and making beds or ridges as per crop requirement.

Seed Treatment:
Treat seeds with fungicides and insecticides before sowing to protect against seed-borne diseases and pests. Use bio-fertilizers like Rhizobium for leguminous crops.

Weed Management:
Weeds compete with crops for nutrients, water, and sunlight. Use cultural, mechanical, and chemical methods for weed control. Pre-emergence and post-emergence herbicides can be used.

Harvesting:
Harvest crops at the right maturity stage for optimum yield and quality. Use proper harvesting tools and techniques to minimize losses.

Post-Harvest Management:
Proper storage, drying, and processing are essential to maintain quality and prevent losses. Store grains at appropriate moisture content (12-14%) in clean, dry, and well-ventilated storage structures.
