Integrated Pest Management (IPM) - Comprehensive Guide

APHIDS
Aphids are small, soft-bodied insects that suck plant sap. They multiply rapidly and can transmit viral diseases.

Symptoms:
- Curled and yellowing leaves
- Sticky honeydew on leaves
- Presence of black sooty mold
- Stunted plant growth
- Distorted shoots and flowers

Management:
1. Cultural: Grow aphid-resistant varieties, use reflective mulches, remove weeds
2. Biological: Encourage ladybugs, lacewings, and parasitic wasps. Release beneficial insects
3. Chemical: Spray neem oil (2-3 ml/liter) or use systemic insecticides like imidacloprid (only when threshold is crossed)

Threshold: 5-10 aphids per leaf or 10% infested plants

WHITE FLY
Whiteflies are small, white-winged insects that feed on plant sap and transmit viral diseases.

Symptoms:
- Yellowing and wilting of leaves
- Sticky honeydew secretion
- Sooty mold development
- Stunted growth
- Leaf drop

Management:
1. Cultural: Use yellow sticky traps, remove infested leaves, maintain field hygiene
2. Biological: Release Encarsia formosa, Eretmocerus spp.
3. Chemical: Spray imidacloprid (0.5 ml/liter) or thiamethoxam (0.3 g/liter)

Threshold: 5-10 adults per leaf

THRIPS
Thrips are tiny, slender insects that feed on plant tissues and transmit tospoviruses.

Symptoms:
- Silver or bronze streaks on leaves
- Deformed flowers and fruits
- Stunted growth
- Leaf curling

Management:
1. Cultural: Use blue sticky traps, remove weeds, maintain field hygiene
2. Biological: Release predatory mites (Amblyseius spp.)
3. Chemical: Spray spinosad (0.3 ml/liter) or fipronil (1 ml/liter)

Threshold: 5-10 thrips per leaf

CATERPILLARS AND LEAF EATERS
Various caterpillars like armyworms, cutworms, and leaf folders damage crops by feeding on leaves.

Symptoms:
- Holes in leaves
- Skeletonized leaves
- Defoliation
- Damage to fruits and pods

Management:
1. Cultural: Hand picking, use light traps, maintain field hygiene
2. Biological: Release Trichogramma spp., use Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) formulations
3. Chemical: Spray emamectin benzoate (0.5 g/liter) or chlorantraniliprole (0.3 ml/liter)

Threshold: 5-10% leaf damage

STEM BORERS
Stem borers are serious pests of rice, maize, and sugarcane that bore into stems.

Symptoms:
- Dead hearts in rice (central shoot dries up)
- Holes in stems
- Wilting and drying of plants
- Reduced yield

Management:
1. Cultural: Remove stubbles, use resistant varieties, adjust sowing time
2. Biological: Release Trichogramma chilonis, use Bt formulations
3. Chemical: Apply cartap hydrochloride granules or spray chlorantraniliprole

Threshold: 5% dead hearts or 1-2 egg masses per plant

RUST DISEASES
Rust diseases are caused by fungal pathogens and appear as orange-brown pustules on leaves and stems.

Types:
- Leaf rust (brown rust)
- Stem rust (black rust)
- Stripe rust (yellow rust)

Symptoms:
- Yellow, orange, or brown pustules on leaves
- Premature leaf drop
- Reduced grain filling
- Stunted growth

Management:
1. Use resistant varieties
2. Remove and destroy infected plant debris
3. Apply fungicides like propiconazole (0.1%) or tebuconazole (0.1%) at first sign of disease
4. Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization
5. Crop rotation

BLAST DISEASE
Blast is a serious fungal disease affecting rice, wheat, and other cereals.

Symptoms:
- Diamond-shaped lesions on leaves
- Node and neck blast in rice
- Reduced grain filling
- Complete crop failure in severe cases

Management:
1. Use resistant varieties
2. Avoid excessive nitrogen application
3. Apply fungicides like tricyclazole (0.6 g/liter) or isoprothiolane
4. Maintain proper spacing
5. Remove infected plant debris

WILT DISEASES
Wilt diseases are caused by soil-borne fungi that block the vascular system.

Symptoms:
- Wilting and yellowing of leaves
- Stunted growth
- Brown discoloration of vascular tissues
- Plant death

Management:
1. Use resistant varieties
2. Soil solarization
3. Crop rotation with non-host crops
4. Apply bio-fungicides like Trichoderma viride
5. Avoid waterlogging

LEAF SPOT DISEASES
Various fungal pathogens cause leaf spot diseases in different crops.

Symptoms:
- Circular or irregular spots on leaves
- Yellow halos around spots
- Premature defoliation
- Reduced photosynthesis

Management:
1. Use disease-free seeds
2. Remove infected plant debris
3. Apply fungicides like mancozeb (0.25%) or carbendazim (0.1%)
4. Maintain proper spacing for air circulation
5. Avoid overhead irrigation

DOWNY MILDEW
Downy mildew affects many vegetable crops and is characterized by white fungal growth on leaf undersides.

Symptoms:
- Yellow patches on upper leaf surface
- White to grayish fungal growth on lower surface
- Premature defoliation
- Reduced yield

Management:
1. Use resistant varieties
2. Avoid overhead irrigation
3. Apply fungicides like metalaxyl (0.1%) or mancozeb (0.25%)
4. Maintain proper spacing
5. Remove infected plant parts

POWDERY MILDEW
Powdery mildew appears as white powdery growth on leaves, stems, and fruits.

Symptoms:
- White powdery patches on leaves
- Yellowing and premature leaf drop
- Reduced photosynthesis
- Poor fruit quality

Management:
1. Use resistant varieties
2. Apply fungicides like sulfur (0.2%) or hexaconazole (0.1%)
3. Maintain proper spacing
4. Remove infected plant parts
5. Avoid excessive nitrogen

ROOT KNOT NEMATODES
Nematodes are microscopic worms that attack plant roots, causing galls and stunted growth.

Symptoms:
- Galls or knots on roots
- Stunted growth
- Yellowing of leaves
- Reduced yield

Management:
1. Use resistant varieties
2. Crop rotation with marigold or mustard
3. Soil solarization
4. Apply bio-nematicides like Paecilomyces lilacinus
5. Use organic amendments

INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM) PRINCIPLES

1. Prevention:
- Use healthy, certified seeds
- Maintain field hygiene
- Remove weeds and crop residues
- Practice crop rotation

2. Monitoring:
- Regular field scouting
- Use pheromone traps
- Monitor pest populations
- Keep records

3. Cultural Control:
- Select resistant varieties
- Adjust sowing time
- Maintain proper spacing
- Use trap crops
- Intercropping

4. Biological Control:
- Conserve natural enemies
- Release beneficial insects
- Use bio-pesticides
- Maintain biodiversity

5. Chemical Control:
- Use pesticides only when necessary
- Follow economic threshold levels
- Rotate different modes of action
- Use selective pesticides
- Follow proper application methods

6. Evaluation:
- Monitor effectiveness
- Adjust strategies
- Document results
- Continuous improvement

ECONOMIC THRESHOLD LEVELS (ETL)

- Aphids: 5-10 per leaf or 10% infested plants
- Whitefly: 5-10 adults per leaf
- Thrips: 5-10 per leaf
- Leaf folders: 5-10% leaf damage
- Stem borers: 5% dead hearts
- Rust: 5% leaf area affected
- Blast: 1% leaf area affected

PESTICIDE APPLICATION GUIDELINES

1. Read label instructions carefully
2. Use recommended doses
3. Apply at right time (early morning or evening)
4. Use proper equipment
5. Follow safety precautions
6. Avoid application during flowering (to protect pollinators)
7. Maintain pre-harvest intervals
8. Rotate different pesticides
9. Dispose of containers properly
10. Keep records of applications

SAFETY MEASURES

1. Wear protective clothing
2. Use masks and gloves
3. Avoid eating, drinking, or smoking during application
4. Wash hands and body after application
5. Store pesticides safely
6. Keep away from children and animals
7. Follow first aid measures if exposed
8. Seek medical help if needed
